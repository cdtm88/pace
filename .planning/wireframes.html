Pace · low-fi wireframes

generate → ride → log

An exploration of the screens behind Pace — the AI cycling coach that builds a session from your readiness, shows it glanceably on the bike, and logs it back from Strava. Structure & flow only; visuals come later.

accent = live / active / current effort dashed = optional or empty state ✎ captions = design rationale
Tweaks swap the accent, sketch level, hand-font & toggle notes →
01

Onboarding wizard

Three quick steps. FTP & weight are optional — the app never blocks you for not knowing your numbers.

02

Dashboard / home

The hub. Shown in both states the app supports — with FTP (power zones) and without (feel-based RPE).

03

Readiness → generate

A single 0–3 tap sets the tone of today's AI session. Plus the loading and safe-fallback states.

04

Today view — on the bike

The hero. Read at arm's length, mid-effort, sweaty. Four directions for showing what to do right now. Big watt target + zone/RPE are the constants; they differ on how much structure & context they surface.

05

Pre-ride preview & .zwo export

Before clipping in: what you're in for, and a one-tap Zwift export. Shown with FTP (TSS) and without (duration + intensity).

06

Strava & progress

Connect once, rides auto-match by date/duration, and a rolling 6-week TSS chart closes the loop. Includes the 429 “can't reach Strava” state.
